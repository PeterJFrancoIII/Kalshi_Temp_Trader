# Kalshi KMIA Nomenclature & Terminology Governance

This document serves as the canonical glossary and terminology standard for the Kalshi KMIA project. All subsystem agents, developers, and code interfaces must adhere to these definitions to prevent errors in mapping, probability modeling, and risk assessment.

> [!CAUTION]
> **SAFETY MANDATE:** This project is strictly for **DRY-RUN / PAPER EVALUATION ONLY**. There are no real trading executions. Methods such as `create_order`, `submit_order`, or `place_order` are strictly forbidden across the codebase. See `docs/REAL_TRADING_GATE.md` for enforcement details.

---

## 1. Kalshi Ticker and Settlement Boundary Examples

* **Kalshi Ticker:** The full identifier for a Kalshi contract (e.g., `KXHIGHMIA-26MAY08-B86`).
* **Event Ticker:** The identifier for the market suite corresponding to a specific day (e.g., `KXHIGHMIA-26MAY08`).
* **Target Date / Event Date:** The canonical local calendar date for the prediction (e.g., `2026-05-13`). Always evaluated in the US/Eastern timezone to avoid UTC rollover issues.
* **Condition Type:** The parsed directional classification of a contract: `above`, `below`, `between`, or `unknown`.
* **Threshold / Boundary (`threshold_f`, `range_high_f`):** The numerical temperature boundaries for a given contract.
* **Contract Range:** The human-readable string representing the logical range (e.g., `>86`, `<=84`, `91-92`).
* **Forecast Bin Label (`forecast_bin_label`):** The canonical string representing the threshold boundary.
* **Fallback Used (`fallback_used`):** Boolean indicating if a multi-contract inference fallback was used for parsing.
* **Parse Warnings (`parse_warnings`):** List of warnings generated during ticker parsing.

**Explicit Integer Boundary Examples:**
* `KXHIGHMIA-26MAY15-T92`: Means `>92°F`. Canonical integer label: `>=93`. UI label: `≥93.0°F` or `≥93°F`.
* `KXHIGHMIA-26MAY15-T85` with title `<85°`: Means `<=84`. Canonical integer label: `<=84`. UI label: `≤84.0°F` or `≤84°F`.
* `KXHIGHMIA-26MAY15-B91.5`: Means `91-92°F` (inclusive 91 and 92).

> [!NOTE]
> The `-T` suffix in a ticker does not universally dictate whether a contract is "above" or "below". Parsing relies on strike properties, titles, and multi-contract fallbacks.

## 2. Weather and Observation Terms

* **Current Temp (`current_temp_f`):** The real-time current temperature reported by the NWS/TWC.
* **Observed Max So Far (`observed_max_so_far_f`):** The real-time NWS high temperature recorded so far today. Any forecast bins terminating below this value strictly receive zero probability.
* **Latest Observation Time (`latest_observation_time`):** The timestamp of the most recent weather observation from NWS/TWC.
* **Fetched At UTC (`fetched_at_utc`):** The UTC timestamp when the data snapshot was retrieved from the external API.
* **Settlement Truth / Official Final Max:** The final, official daily maximum temperature reported by the NWS which Kalshi uses to settle the markets.

## 3. Forecast and Distribution Terms

* **Forecast High (`forecast_high_f`):** The predicted maximum temperature from the deterministic forecast.
* **Deterministic Anchor (`deterministic_anchor_f`):** The central high temperature used to center the probability distribution.
* **Integer Distribution (`integer_distribution`):** The foundational, 1°F-resolution probability distribution generated by the weather models (e.g., `{ 84: 0.1, 85: 0.5, 86: 0.4 }`).
* **Model Probability (`model_probability`):** The final predicted probability for a specific contract, computed by integrating the `integer_distribution` over the contract's boundaries.

## 4. Market Price / Implied Probability Terms

* **Yes Ask (`yes_ask`) / Yes Bid (`yes_bid`):** The current top-of-book prices in cents (e.g., 45 means $0.45). `yes_ask` is conventionally used to model the worst-case buy execution.
* **Last Price (`last_price`):** The last traded price in cents, used as a fallback.
* **Executable Price:** The price selected by the system for paper trading edge calculations (prioritizes `yes_ask` and falls back to `last_price`).
* **Market Probability (`market_probability`):** The market's implied probability based on the executable price.
* **Yes Ask Implied Probability (`yes_ask_implied_probability`):** Specifically the implied probability derived purely from the `yes_ask` price (`yes_ask / 100.0`).
* **Breakeven Probability (`breakeven_probability`):** The true probability required to break even after factoring in fees and slippage.

## 5. Risk and Paper Signal Terms

* **Signal Generator:** The sub-system that merges the forecast distribution, market snapshot, and real-time orderbooks to evaluate edge and risk.
* **Raw Edge (`raw_edge`):** The unadjusted difference: `model_probability - market_probability`.
* **Fee-Adjusted Edge (`edge`):** The refined edge metric incorporating trading fees and slippage models.
* **Expected Value (`expected_value`):** The statistical expected value of a contract based on model probability and payout.
* **Paper Action:** The final recommendation for a contract. Valid states: `PAPER BUY CANDIDATE`, `WATCH`, `BLOCKED BY RISK ENGINE`, `NO SIGNAL`.
* **Risk Decision:** A structured evaluation object consisting of `passed`, `reason`, `failed_gate_id`, `failed_gate_name`.
* **Fail-Closed:** The foundational risk architecture blocking trades on missing/stale data.
* **As Of Time (`as_of_time`):** The point-in-time timestamp for backtesting or signaling to prevent lookahead leakage.
* **Lookahead Leakage:** The critical safety violation of using future data (e.g., end-of-day highs) to make past trading decisions.
* **Replay Manifest (`replay_manifest.json`):** The log recording accepted and rejected simulation days during backtesting.

## 8. Do Not Confuse Table

| Term A | Term B | Distinction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `model_probability` | `market_probability` | `model_probability` is our internal forecast. `market_probability` is derived from external Kalshi orderbooks. |
| `raw_edge` | `edge` | `raw_edge` is unadjusted difference. `edge` factors in Kalshi fees and slippage models. |
| `current_temp_f` | `observed_max_so_far_f` | `current_temp_f` is the current temperature. `observed_max_so_far_f` is the highest temperature recorded so far today. |
| `forecast_high_f` | `deterministic_anchor_f` | `forecast_high_f` is the raw NWS high. `deterministic_anchor_f` is the specific center point chosen for the probability distribution. |
| `-T92` | `>92°F` | A `-T92` ticker *might* mean `>92°F` or `<92°F`. Never assume solely based on the `-T` suffix. |
| `as_of_time` | `fetched_at_utc` | `fetched_at_utc` is when data was literally downloaded. `as_of_time` represents the simulated "now" during backtesting to prevent lookahead leakage. |
